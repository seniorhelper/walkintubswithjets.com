What You Need to Know

Why a Walk-In Tub With Jets Is Different From a Regular Bath

A walk-in tub with jets combines two things that matter most to seniors and people with limited mobility: a safe way to get in and out, and therapeutic warm water with movement. The watertight door means no climbing over a high tub wall. The jets mean warm water actively works around your body — not just passively sitting in it.

Warm water immersion and gentle hydrotherapy have been used for centuries in spas, rehabilitation settings, and medical facilities. These tubs bring that experience into your own bathroom — with the safety features that standard bathtubs simply don't have.

  • 🚪
    Watertight Door EntryStep in and sit down before filling the tub. The door seals completely — no spillage, no climbing.
  • 💧
    Hydrotherapy JetsAir jets, whirlpool jets, or both move warm water around your body for a therapeutic soak.
  • 🌡️
    Inline Water HeaterKeeps your bath warm from start to finish — critical for extended soaking sessions.
  • 🛡️
    Anti-Scald ControlsThermostatic valve prevents water from getting dangerously hot — important for seniors with reduced sensitivity.
Choosing Your Jet System

Air Jets vs. Whirlpool Jets vs. Combination Systems

The jet system is the most important decision you'll make. Each type feels completely different. Here's what to know before you call.

🫧

Air Hydrotherapy Jets

Hundreds of tiny holes in the tub floor and sides release warm air bubbles that rise around your entire body. Most people describe it as sitting in a bath of gentle champagne bubbles. It's a soft, full-body sensation — not a focused stream.

Best for: Sensitive skin, full-body relaxation, those who prefer gentle therapy. Easiest to clean and maintain.

🌊 Gentle & Full-Body
💦

Whirlpool Water Jets

Pressurized water streams shoot from larger nozzles that can be rotated and directed toward specific areas — calves, lower back, hips, shoulders. It feels more like a traditional spa jet massage. Stronger and more targeted than air jets.

Best for: Targeted muscle relief, those who prefer a stronger massage effect. Great for back and joint discomfort.

🎯 Focused & Targeted
⭐

Combination Jet Systems

Get the best of both worlds. Run air jets alone for a gentle effervescent soak, whirlpool jets alone for focused massage, or both at once for the most immersive experience. Most popular option among our customers.

Best for: Maximum flexibility. You control the experience every time. Ideal for households with different preferences.

Safety & Comfort Features on Every Walk-In Tub

A great walk-in tub isn't just about jets. Every model we install comes equipped with these essential safety and comfort features.

01

Low Step-In Door Threshold

Most walk-in tub doors have a 3–7 inch step-in height vs. 14–18 inches on a standard tub. Low-threshold models at 3–4 inches are available for those with minimal leg lift.

02

Slip-Resistant Floor Texture

ADA-rated textured flooring dramatically reduces fall risk even when wet. Far safer than standard smooth tub surfaces.

03

Built-In Grab Bars

Strategically positioned grab bars anchored to support safe transfers in and out of the tub at every critical point.

04

Contoured Seat

Ergonomic built-in seat at the right height for comfortable, safe seated bathing without any add-on equipment.

05

Inline Water Heater

Keeps bath water at your preferred temperature throughout the entire soak — essential since you enter before filling and exit after draining.

06

Handheld Shower Wand

Height-adjustable wand on a slide bar for rinsing, hair washing, or showering — usable from the seat or standing.

An inline water heater is a built-in heating element inside the walk-in tub that keeps bath water at your preferred temperature throughout your soak. Because walk-in tubs require you to enter before filling and exit after draining, you spend considerably more time in the tub than with a standard bath. Without an inline heater, the water can cool significantly during that extended time. This feature is strongly recommended — particularly for users who benefit from longer hydrotherapy sessions for joint pain or muscle soreness.
Air jet systems are easiest to maintain — since they only circulate air (not water) through the pipes, there's no risk of bacteria buildup in the jet lines. Whirlpool jet systems should be purged monthly by running a cleaning solution (like a tub cleaner or diluted bleach) through the jets for 15 minutes. The tub surface should be cleaned with non-abrasive cleaners to protect the acrylic finish. Door seals should be inspected annually.
